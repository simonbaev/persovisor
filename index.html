Example: Business Trip
from Boston to New York

The Problem

Mr. John Doe has to attend a meeting in New York at 3:00 PM tomorrow. He lives in Boston and decides to take a non-stop flight from Logan International Airport (Boston) that is scheduled to depart at 7:00 AM. This flight is expected to arrive to LaGuardia Airport (New York) at 8:20 AM.

Upon arrival to the airport, Mr. Doe finds out that his flight is delayed due to weather conditions in New York City. He needs to make an adjustment to his plans and the following are his options:

  1. Option 1. Cancel the flight and reschedule the meeting for another day ("Cancel").
  2. Option 2. Wait until the weather in New York City will get better and the flight will be rescheduled to depart no later than by noon ("Wait").
  3. Option 3. Drive a rental car from Boston to New York. It should take approximately four hours without traffic ("Drive").

Metrics

Each of these options is associated with its own pros (+) and cons (-) which have their corresponding intensity (I) and a likelihood (L) to happen. Both metrics (i.e. intensity and likelihood) are measured in levels from 1 (lowest) to 7 (highest).

Cancel

Pros (+) This option would allow Mr. Doe to better prepare for the meeting and attend it later (I=4, L=7).

Cons (-) This option could delay resolution of a number of urgent problems that are planned for discussion on the meeting (I=5, L=6).

Wait

Pros (+) Waiting is the easiest solution (I=6, L=5).

Cons (-) Waiting too long could cancel other options (I=5, L=5).

Drive

Pros (+) Driving is a pleasure, no turbulence, no need to rent a car in New York (I=6, L=6).

Cons (-) Weather conditions could result in a heavy traffic, so to an increased danger (I=7, L=2).

Result

The proposed method ranks given options in the following order of preference: "Drive", "Wait", and "Cancel".

About ExpressDecision

Slogan

A fast and easy way to make the right decision

Description

ExpressDecision is a compact web application designed to help you to choose among a number of most favorable options. It takes into account psychological patterns that are identified on the basis of input provided by a user.

How does it work?

In the Personalization tab (next one to the right), you will need to specify an arbitrary set of options from which you are hesitating to choose one. In that tab you will only need to name them, so that later on, in the Play tab, you will be able to distinguish them by name. Tabs can be clicked (tapped), or if you are working on a mobile device (phone, tablet, phablet, etc) you can also perform a swipe gesture to the left or to the right to switch tabs as necessary.

During the play phase you will need to think about each option as having its own pros (+) and cons (-), with a corresponding level of intensity and a likelihood to happen.

Intensity would be associated with a level measured from extremely weak to extremely strong, and likelihood would be associated with some other level measured from extremely seldom to extremely often. Both levels can be set by dragging corresponding slider handles. The value of each metric can be controlled by observing color of the slider handle as well as a label displayed on top of the slider. Default value of the label is set to >>> to indicate that selection of intensity and likelihood has yet to be made.

As soon as the winner is identified, corresponding option on the play tab will be highlighted. In some cases a tide is possible, so two or more (never all) options would be highlighted.

What are your options?

Please list at least three options that you are considering, but with which you are having difficulty to select the best choice. We need three or more (probably no more than five) options to avoid situations in which the pros of one option are implicitly linked to the cons of another and vice versa.

Instructions

Drag each slider to balance the positive / negative outcomes of each option and their likelihoods. Any significant change of sliders positions will trigger re-ranking of all options. As a result, each option will be assigned with a tag, for example 2, to indicate relative standing of this particular option with respect to others. The winner option will be assigned with the tag of 1 and its title area will be highlighted.
